Dissertation Description: Investment and the Transfer of Power: Dynamic Effects of Transmission in Electricity Markets
======
## Renewable Energy and Transmission Challenges:

* Renewable resources are essential for the energy transition, but unequal geographic distribution limits nationwide adoption.
* Long-distance transmission offers a potential solution by connecting renewables-rich areas to high-demand markets.

## Research Focus:
* Examines how expanded transmission capacity impacts investment decisions by generators.

## Dynamic Model Development:

* Develops and estimates a dynamic model of generator behavior consisting of a short-run optimal dispatch problem incorporating line losses and zonal transmission constraints and a long-run dynamic game for capacity investment.

## Dataset:
* Data from ISOs in the Eastern Interconnection and ERCOT from 2018-2023.

## Key Results:
* Upgrading all interzonal transmission to modern HVDC standards would reduce average wholesale prices by 6%, generating approximately $4 billion in annual welfare gains -- roughly one-third of total congestion costs.
* Even partial upgrades (50% of lines) achieve over 5% price reductions.
* Regional effects are heterogeneous: the Northeast experiences the largest price declines while the Midwest sees modest increases.
* Adding major transmission projects such as the Grain Belt Express increases solar and wind adoption in the Eastern Interconnection by over 10% by 2050.
* Transmission and investment subsidies (e.g., Inflation Reduction Act, Bipartisan Infrastructure Law) are complements in encouraging transition.
